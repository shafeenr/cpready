CPReady

The Problem

Here's our take on the solution.

Fifteen to thirty percent of American out-of-hospital cardiac arrest cases recieve any bystander CPR. If CPR can dramatically increase your chances of coming back from a cardiac arrest, why don't more people know and perform CPR?

Experts point out that people cite a lack of time or general disinterest in CPR classes, and students who do take CPR classes usually forget the material they learned and the importance of it. Sometimes people are even frightened that their contributions may make things worse.

In short, many people are inexperienced with CPR or they simply don't want to CPR at all.

That's where we come in.

With the CPReady, users can see the pressure they're exerting their compressions and they can hear the rhythm of which they must commit their compressions at. This can immensly help bystanders inexperienced with CPR.

The CPReady kit can be placed around institutions, arena and other public areas to maximize the effectiveness of the product.
