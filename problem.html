The Problem

Here's our take on the solution.

Fifteen to thirty percent of American out-of-hospital cardiac arrest cases recieve any bystander CPR. If CPR can dramatically increase your chances of coming back from a cardiac arrest, why don't more people know and perform CPR?

Experts point out that people cite a lack of time or general disinterest in CPR classes, and students who do take CPR classes usually forget the material they learned and the importance of it.
